Intro to Non-Parametric Statistics

In real world applications it is often the case that the a dataset's generating process is unknown. Moreover, even assuming the process can at least in principle be expressed in closed form, this might be to complex in nature to allow for suitable approximations. Non-parametric methods allow to do inference and make statistical sense of data when a model for the underlying generating process is not available.
In this sense, non-parametric methods are more general than their parametric counterparts, as they rely on a smaller set of assumptions (e.g. smoothness of the density function, or existence of moments, as opposed to a very specific parametric model specification for the data generating process). Conversely, parametric models trade-off generality for increased efficiency. That is, if the data truly satisfies the assumption on the specific parametric generating process, then the parametric approach provides the most efficient estimators and inferencial procedures.
